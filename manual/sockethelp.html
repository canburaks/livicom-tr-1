Description
The Livi Socket (hereafter referred to as the socket) is designed for remote control of domestic machines and electrical appliance with powers of up to 3.5 kW.

There are two ways to turn on / turn off the appliance plugged into the socket:

  1. On the user command, remotely submitted through the Livicom app.
  2. Automatically, when Livi Smart Hub (hereafter referred to as the hub) runs a script.

The socket is able to work as a thermostat to control electric heaters with powers of up to 3.5 kW. The socket measures instant voltage and power consumption, and transmits the measurements to the hub.

An indicator (1) and a function button (2) are located on the socket enclosure. The indicator allows users seeing whether the socket is on or off. The function button allows turning on and turning off the device without the Livicom app. The socket has a white plastic enclosure. The plug of the socket (3) has earthing contacts. The socket has child-proof socket shutters (4). The socket can be plugged into a CEE 7/4 standard AC outlet.
Socket appearance
  1. LED indicator
  2. Function button
  3. CEE 7/4 standard AC plug with earthing contacts
  4. Child-proof socket shutters



Precautions
Observe the general electrical safety rules and regulations when operating the socket.


DO NOT open the socket enclosure
Binding the socket to the hub
The socket must be unpacked and allowed to reach room temperature for at least two hours before handling if it was transported or stored at low temperatures.
Only the owner of the site can bind the socket to the hub. Make sure that the site guard is disabled and the hub is online before binding.
1. Plug the socket into a CEE 7/4 standard AC outlet with earthing contacts (230 V mains). The socket indicator will start blinking blue once the socket is switched to the binding mode.

2. In the Livicom app, open the "Devices" screen. In the upper right corner of the screen tap "+" and select "Add Device".

The system compiles a list of radio devices available for binding within the range of the hub radio channel.

3. Select the socket from the list and check that its name and serial number in the app match the data on the socket enclosure.

4. Tap CONTINUE and follow on-screen instructions in the app to bind the socket to the hub.

The socket indicator will blink green 5 times after successful binding.

The socket switches to the binding mode only for 60 seconds. If you have not bound it to the hub within this period, plug the socket out of the power outlet for 10 seconds and replug it into the outlet. The socket will switch to binding mode again.
Choosing a location for the socket
The socket is designed to be plugged into a CEE 7/4 standard AC outlet with earthing contacts (230 V mains).

DO NOT mount the socket:

  • outdoors;
  • in metal boxes and electrical panels;
  • in places with high humidity, or at temperatures exceeding the operating temperature range (see "Specifications" table).
Evaluating signal strength
Check the quality of the connection between the socket and the hub at the intended location of the socket. There are two ways to evaluate the signal strength:

  1. In the Livicom app, on the socket settings screen.
  2. With the help of the LED indication on the socket. Double-click on the function button and look at the socket indicator. Interpret the indication using the table below.

Checking the socket operation
Send to the socket a turn on command through the Livicom app to check the socket functionality. Make sure that the power load plugged into the socket is turned on and the socket indicator lights green.

Then send to the socket a turn off command through the Livicom app. Make sure that the power load is turned off and the socket indicator is not lit.

DO NOT use the socket and contact technical support (mail to: support@livicom.ru) if you see an incorrect operation of the socket.
Attention
In case of power failure the socket will turn on automatically after power recovery, if the hub continues to run on battery power and the socket has been turned on before the failure.

The socket will stay turned off after power recovery if the hub stops running due to power loss even if the socket has been turned on before the failure.

DO NOT plug the socket out of the power outlet if the connected device is turned on

Make sure that you have turned off the connected device before plugging the socket out of the 230 V mains.
Socket plate in the Livicom app
Tap on the Devices button in the Livicom app. The socket plate appears in the list on the Devices screen after the socket has been bound to the hub.
The socket plate displays:

1. The name of the socket.

2. A switch that allows to turn on and off the power of the appliance plugged into the socket. Slide the switch to the right to turn on the power. The switch will not be displayed if the Use as a thermostat feature is enabled for the socket (see below).

3. The current status of the socket: Turned on / Turned off or Heats up / Cools down (if the Use as a thermostat feature is enabled).

4. Current power consumed by the socket and the connected electrical appliance.

5. Monthly electricity consumption statistics.

6. Socket icon for quick visual identification.

Tap on the socket plate to open the socket settings screen.
Socket settings screen in the Livicom app
The socket settings screen displays:

  • Socket description:
    • name;
    • appearance;
    • type and serial number;
  • The current status of the socket: Turned on / Turned off or Heats up / Cools down (if the function Use as a thermostat feature is enabled).
  • Monthly electricity consumption statistics.
  • Signal strength - the quality of the connection between the ocket and the hub.
  • Power consumption - the power consumed by the socket and the connected electrical appliance.
  • Line voltage - instantaneous voltage in the AC network, measured by the socket.
  • Socket operational settings:
    • use as a thermostat;
    • power.
  • Group. Tap on the group name to move the socket to another group.

Radio signal strength between the socket and the hub (no connection / poor / average / good)
If there is no connection with the hub, check whether the hub is turned on. If the hub is off, turn the hub on and again check the signal strength on the socket settings screen.

Then check whether there are any objects obstructing radio signal (for example, metal objects or mirrors) between the hub and the socket. Change the socket installation location so that radio signal could pass between devices without obstructions.

Check whether the socket is installed within radio signal range of the hub If the socket is installed too far from the hub, move it to a new place where connection between the socket and the hub will be restored.

The location of the hub also affects the signal strength. In some cases even a slight displacement of the hub or even its rotation gives a significant improvement in a quality of its communication with devices.
    Socket operational settings
    Only the owner of the site can change settings for the socket. Make sure that the site guard is disabled and the hub is online if you are going to change the settings.
    • Use as a thermostat is a switch that allows to turn on and off the Use as a thermostat feature for the socket. Slide the switch to the right to turn on the function. Afterwards the socket will turn on and off automatically to maintain the desired room temperature.

    • Power is a switch that allows to turn on and off the appliance plugged into the socket. Slide the switch to the right to turn on the power. The Power switch will not be displayed If the Use as a thermostat feature is enabled for the socket.
      You can add the socket control button to Favorites or assign it to one of the buttons on the Livi Key Fob control panel after binding the socket to the hub (If the Use as a thermostat feature is disabled for the socket).
      Renaming the socket
      Only the owner of the site can rename the socket. Make sure that the site guard is disabled and the hub is online before renaming.
      • In the upper right corner of the socket settings screen tap on three dots ("Menu" button).
      • Select Rename in the menu.
      • Enter a new name and save it.

      Maintenance
      Keep the socket free of dust and dirt.
      Attention

      Do not wipe the socket with substances containing alcohol, acetone, gasoline and other active solvents
      Deleting the socket (unbinding from the hub)
      Only the owner of the site can unbind the socket from the hub. Make sure that the site guard is disabled and the hub is online before deleting the socket.
      You can delete the socket from the hub using the menu on the socket settings screen.

      If you need to forcefully delete the socket, plug the socket out of the power outlet for 30 seconds. Replug the socket into the outlet and quickly press on the function button until the socket indicator starts blinking blue.

      Warranty
      The manufacturer LLC "NPP Stels" guarantees that the socket meets AGNS.421453.001 TU technical requirements, provided that the consumer complies with the conditions of transportation, storage, installation and operation.

      The warranty period is 5 years from the manufacturing date.

      The warranty does not cover the following cases:

      1. Non-compliance with the intended operating conditions;
      2. Mechanical damage to the socket;
      3. Repairs to the socket by a third party (a person or a company other than the Manufacturer).
